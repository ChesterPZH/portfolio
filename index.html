Vision of Work

Embodied and Intelligent Interactivity - Reimagining Everyday Activities with XR Spatial Computing

"Yet almost all new products reduce interaction to pushing a labeled button They viewed this trend as a failure to account for people as sensual beings; a failure to focus on perceptual motor skills as a source of interaction inspiration, due to an overreliance on cognition as a singular theoretical stance in interaction design, and a failure to consider aesthetics as a critical component of interaction."

Computer interface should reach beyond on-screen clicks to involve touch, hearing, and spatial perception, to truly break such limitations. entended reality (XR) systems realize this ambition by â€œdowngradingâ€ the computer interface, blending it directly into everyday 3D world activity. Such systems let users apply their natural spatial perceptual abilities, including depth, orientation, coordinated motor movement, to interact with augmented virtual content while still engaging with the physical world, to move beyond the constraint of passive screens.

My primary research goal is to re-imagine human activities through XR spatial computing, highlighting our innate spatial perceptual routines, and to create tools, interfaces, and experiences that support these activities.

Tangible Compass: An Affordable and DIY-Friendly Handheld Kinesthetic Device for Haptic Accessible Navigation

Pan, Zhaohan, Ge, G., Jiang, Z., Meng, Z., Wang, L., Wu, H., Wang, L., Liang, J., Wen, H.

In Proceedings of ACM CHCHI '24

Aim to design a low-cost, open-source device providing additional sensory feedback to support accessible navigation, we introduce Tangible Compass, a handheld haptic device as an add-on for smartphone navigation functions, providing force guidance in various accessible navigation scenarios.

Its functionality is achieved through smartphone sensor data streaming, scenario-based algorithmic force rendering, and a compact one Degree of Freedom (1-DoF) kinesthetic device transmitting forces. We featured interaction scenarios like route orientating navigation and clearance detection.
